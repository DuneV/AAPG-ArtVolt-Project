The Problem

Rural Paipa's energy
and water crisis

Three veredas (hamlets) around Paipa face compounding challenges that no single existing solution addresses.

🧂

Salinized Water

Local water is salinized by the hydrothermal flow of the Paipa geothermal anomaly — salinity of 20,000–60,000 mg/L makes it unusable for humans or agriculture.

⚡

No Reliable Energy

The electrical grid doesn't reach the area. Existing pumps run on gasoline or hybrid batteries. Each hamlet runs its own independent aqueduct, unregulated by the municipality.

🔥

Firewood Cooking

Families cook with firewood, causing respiratory disease and loss of productive hours, with no infrastructure for gas or electricity as healthier alternatives.

🤝

Distrust in Providers

Energy providers have repeatedly failed to deliver, lack community relationships, and have no real presence in the territory. Communities no longer believe outside solutions will work.

Our Solution

One compact tower,
three complementary sources

ArVolt integrates three renewable energy sources into a single modular tower, delivering 24/7 autonomous power for water pumping and community use.

☀️

Solar Tracking Crown

Six radial panels with single-motor tracker: +27.1% annual yield vs. fixed — validated in simulation.

💨

Vertical-Axis Turbine

NACA 0018 blades keep generating at night and through the rainy season.

🌡️

TEG / Biogas Base

Converts shallow ground heat or farm waste into steady baseline power — no single-point failure.

Autonomous & Reliable

Hybrid generation removes the intermittency risk of solar-only systems. Power flows day, night, sun or rain.

🧩

Modular by Design

The base swaps between geothermal TEG and biogas to fit each site's natural resources. No one-size-fits-all.

📱

Community in Control

A simple app shows available energy and lets locals decide when to pump — giving communities real agency over their water supply.

📡

Data-Generating Asset

Every tower captures environmental and geophysical data in real time, creating a secondary revenue stream via data licensing.

Business Model & Pricing

B2G — the public entity
is the customer

Rural communities receive pumped water at no direct cost. Municipalities, governors' offices, and environmental agencies (like Corpoboyacá) are the paying clients.

🤝

Infrastructure Sales

B2G contracts for modular towers via public procurement and innovation funds. Each sale includes installation and commissioning.

🔧

O&M Contracts

Recurring maintenance agreements with municipalities — predictable, long-term income stream per installed unit.

🗂️

Data Licensing

Environmental and geophysical data captured in real time by each tower, licensed to research and environmental agencies.

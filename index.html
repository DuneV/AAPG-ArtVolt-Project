The Problem

Rural Paipa's compounding crisis

Three veredas around Paipa face interlinked challenges that no existing provider has solved.

🧂

Salinized Water

The Paipa geothermal anomaly raises salinity to 20,000–60,000 mg/L — unusable for humans or agriculture.

⚡

No Grid Access

The electrical grid doesn't reach the area. Pumps run on diesel or hybrid batteries. Each hamlet manages its own unregulated aqueduct.

🔥

Firewood Cooking

Families cook with firewood, causing respiratory disease and lost productive hours — no gas or electricity infrastructure exists.

🤝

Provider Distrust

Energy providers have failed to deliver repeatedly and have no community relationship. People no longer expect external solutions to work.

Our Solution

One compact tower,
three complementary sources

ArVolt integrates solar, wind, and geothermal/biogas into a single modular tower — no single point of failure, 24/7 autonomous generation.

☀️

Solar Tracking Crown

Six radial panels, single-motor tracker: +27.1% annual yield vs. fixed.

💨

Vertical-Axis Turbine

NACA 0018 blades — generates at night and through the rainy season.

🌡️

TEG / Biogas Base

Converts ground heat or farm waste into steady baseline power.

Business Model

B2G — the public entity
is the customer

Rural communities receive pumped water at no direct cost. Municipalities, governors' offices, and environmental agencies are the paying clients.

🤝

Infrastructure Sales

B2G contracts for modular towers via public procurement and innovation funds, including installation and commissioning.

🔧

O&M Contracts

Recurring maintenance agreements with municipalities — a predictable long-term income stream per installed unit.

📊

Data Licensing

Environmental and geophysical data captured in real time by each tower, licensed to research and government agencies.
